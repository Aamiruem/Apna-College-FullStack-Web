Spider-Man

About Spider-Man

Spider-Man is a superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer-editor Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko, he first appeared in the anthology comic book Amazing Fantasy #15 (August 1962) during the Silver Age of Comic Books. Spider-Man has since been featured in comic books, television shows, films, video games, novels, and plays.

Publication Info

  • Publisher: Marvel Comics
  • First Appearance: Amazing Fantasy #15
  • Created by:
    • Stan Lee (Writer)
    • Steve Ditko (Artist)

Creation & Development

In 1962, with the success of the Fantastic Four, Marvel Comics editor and head writer Stan Lee was developing a new superhero concept. The idea for Spider-Man originated from:

  • A surge in teenage demand for comic books
  • The desire to create a relatable teenage protagonist
  • Lee's observation of a spider climbing a wall (as frequently recounted)
  • Inspiration from the pulp magazine crime fighter "The Spider"

In his autobiography, Lee noted that Spider-Man allowed him to address what he felt was missing in comic books at the time - a hero with everyday problems who wasn't perfect.
